Trivias about Jose Rizal’s LIFE, WORKS, and DEATH.

Jose Rizal was a naughty boy in the famous “Moth and Lamp” story.

The young Jose Rizal experimented with drugs.

There are three animals named after Rizal

The Rizal monument in Luneta was not made by a Filipino artist.

He was already a terrific sculptor even at a very young age.

Jose Rizal’s “El Ermitaño.”

Jose Rizal’s anti-Chinese sentiment.

Rizal is regarded by many as the “Father of Philippine Comics” because of some humorous illustrations he made in Germany.

Jose Rizal and the Christmas tree.

Rizal was widely featured in cigarette wrappers. Most of these wrappers, made with Art Deco or Art Nouveau designs, were produced in Binondo during the turn-of-the-century. An old cigarette label, Las Delicias, even went to the extreme by featuring Rizal smoking a cigar.

Not all of Rizal’s skeletal remains were buried under the monument in Luneta. A vertebra or a piece of the backbone where Rizal was allegedly hit by the bullet was claimed by his family and is now displayed at the Rizal Shrine, Fort Santiago.

Jose Rizal’s last words.

Rizal’s original execution photo features a dog, the mascot of the firing squad. It is said that the dog ran around the corpse whining after a soldier fired one last shot in Rizal’s head to make sure he was dead.

He left specific instructions on how he wanted to be treated after his death.

Rizal’s execution led to the death of a Spanish Prime Minister.
